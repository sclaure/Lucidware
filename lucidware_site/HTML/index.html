Lucidware is a technology startup that aims to expand the functionality of wearables. The company’s current project, Reach, is a glove and armband system that captures motion data and recognizes gestures. These data inputs can then be used to empower a variety of applications.

Check out a quick demonstration of the capabilities of Reach!

OUR UNIQUE APPROACH

  • Hand

    Including an inertial measurement unit, flex sensors, and capacitive touch sensors, Talon will accurately display and record each of your movements, rotations, bends, and touches.

  • Forearm

    Containing it's own inertial measurement unit, the forearm component allows for more than just hand tracking, while also working as a reference point for more precise hand measurements

  • Upper Arm

    Housing a battery, inertial measurement unit, and the main computer chip, most of the communication and power stem from the upper arm freeing up the rest of your arm for a lighter experience.

OUR GOALS

A Comfortable Wearable Device...

Our first wearable device, “Reach,” was built for the everyday hacker. Reach is being designed for you to interface with computers, light machinery, and the virtual world in ways that you never thought were possible. But perhaps most importantly, Reach is a comfortable addition to whatever system you’re building.

...Providing A Simply Superior Solution

Our competitors are focused on developing motion-tracking tools, but their solutions are limited in scope. Because our competitors are reliant on cameras for data collection, they can’t provide the accuracy or reliability of our product, Reach.

  • Light Machinery

    Our product, Reach, can be used to intuitively control light machinery. As an example, Reach could allow a user to fly his or her drone through simple hand gestures and/or arm motions.

  • Healthcare

    As a comfortable wearable, Reach can be used as a tool for functional diagnostics. Also, Reach can expand one’s ability in virtual environments, which could be especially useful for virtual surgical training.

  • Gaming

    With its precise and integrated sensors, Reach can make gaming more natural than ever. Users can fully immerse in a video game, as they control a character’s arms/hands with their arms/hands.

  • Interface Control

    Reach allows designers and technologists to intuitively interact with their computers. Move your mouse with an arm gesture, or rotate a shape in AutoCad with the “twisting a lightbulb” motion.

  • Internet of Things

    As a connected device, Reach can be used to interact with an Internet of Things environment. The wearable can make it easy to turn on and off all of the technology around you.

  • Virtual and Artificial Reality

    Reach is the ultimate complement to any VR/AR display. Now, individuals can use their arms and hands in any virtual application.

  • Sports Training

    With the ability to track the smallest changes in arm rotation and movement, Reach is suitable for precise arm motion capture to fine tune any athlete's swing, throw, or movement
