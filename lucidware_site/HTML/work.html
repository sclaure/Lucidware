Prototypes

November 2014

Our journey begins. After dozens of hours researching materials, designing circuit boards, and drawing on AutoCad, Reach turned from a concept into a well-mapped plan.

January 2015

We dive head first into the code. Jack starts developing the backend that would allow Reach to track motion, measure gestures, and process data in real time

January 2015

The team gets to building. Jake finds himself busy engineering the sensors and circuitry of our first Reach prototype.

March 2015

We rebuild the prototype. While our first version worked well, we began streamlining the design for both practicality and comfort.

Today

We’ve got the second prototype finished. We’re now starting our third build and creating new demo applications.
